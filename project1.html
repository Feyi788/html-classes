About me


First things first

Husband, father, son, brother, uncle, friend, mentor, type 1 diabetic, INTJ, porcupine.

All my family comes from Cincinnati

I was born in the suburbs of New Haven, Connecticut, and raised in Nothern Virginia, just outside the Capital Beltway

Now I live in Seattle with my wife, our new kid, and our dog Olive.


Professional background

I'm in the business of making things sense. My mother might tell you i'm a graphic designer, or maybe a web designer-and she's not wrong. There's just more to it than that.

I spent the formative years of my career with small design studios and strategic communications firms serving nonprofits and misson-driven organizations like American Society of Civil Engineers, Anne E. Casey Foundation, Human Rights Campaign, National Association of Independent Schools, and Ploughshares Fund.

I also held positions inside larger organizations. First with National Journal Group (subsidiary of The Atlantic) the DC-based media platform and research consultancy. Then with Seattle Indian Health Board, a federally qualified health center and urban Indian Health program serving American Indian and Alaska Native people across the country.

Nowadays I use design thinking to help the people and organizations grow and do things in their communities. You can hire me to help with practially anything.


Formal Education

I hold a BFA with honors in Graphic Design from the Corcoran College of Art + Design in Washington, DC (before it became part of George Washington University). My senior thesis explored the benefits and pitfalls of three budding social impact programs.

Before pursuing an education in design, I spent two years at Miami University of Ohio studying literature and creative writing. That short time left a big impression, though, and i still harbor a strong urge to write.


Personal interests

When i'm not taking care of the kid, i'm throwing the frisbee for Olive or busy being a Big BrotherTM. I like stage comedy, artsy horror movies, home organization projects, bouldering, ping pong, roller skating, woodworking ,ice hockey, coming up with band games, and collecting flea market dinosaur figurines.
